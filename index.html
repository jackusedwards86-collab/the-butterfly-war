Chapter 1: Day Zero

Posted: February 26, 2024 | Reading Time: ~8 min

*Note: This is the raw opening excerpt that started the book's journey.*

The first seizure came on a cold Sunday at the end of February. Until then, the year had actually started pretty well. I'd done Dry January and felt so good I carried on into February. I was healthy, optimistic, and finally starting to get a handle on being a dad of two. Life felt busy, sure, but balanced.

That afternoon, I'd been upstairs in my office, jotting down some positive actions for Monday. Little did I know, I wouldn't return to work for over a week.

It was almost dinner. I'd put Landon on the table in his rocker, something I knew I shouldn't really do, but he was safely in the middle. We were all together: Louis, three at the time, was in his highchair munching away, Emma nearby. My default mode as a dad is clown; I live for the sound of my kids laughing, jumping, giggling at something silly. And at that moment, that's what I was doing: clowning about, pulling faces, teasing laughter out of Louis, who was giggling and chewing all at once.

Then, in the playful chaos, I turned to Landon and exclaimed 'BOOM'

He blinked. [Image of a butterfly]

I'd startled him, I thought, only something was strange. He'd blinked with just one eye. Confused, I stared. Five seconds passed. Then he blinked again, just the same eye, but this time his tiny body gave a jerk. Not rhythmic, not violent, but unmistakably wrong.

My chest tightened instantly. I felt the words rise from my gut: "Emma, what's wrong with Landon?"

And that was it. The moment the old life ended and the butterfly war began.
